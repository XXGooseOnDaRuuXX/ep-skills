# Copywriting — Conversion Frameworks

## Quick Reference

**Rule #1:** Benefits over features. Always.
**Rule #2:** One page, one goal, one CTA.
**Rule #3:** Write for scanners first, readers second.

## Frameworks

### PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solution)

The workhorse. Works for almost any landing page.

1. **Problem** — Name the pain your audience feels. Be specific. "Managing 50 properties with spreadsheets" not "business is hard."
2. **Agitate** — Twist the knife. Show the consequences of inaction. Lost revenue, wasted time, competitive disadvantage.
3. **Solution** — Present your product as the relief. Connect directly to the agitated pain.

```
Problem:  Your engineering team spends 40% of their time on manual deploys.
Agitate:  That's 2 full days per week not building features. Your competitors
          ship daily while you're debugging CI pipelines.
Solution: [Product] automates your entire deployment pipeline. Push to main,
          we handle the rest. Your team gets those 2 days back.
```

### AIDA (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action)

Best for longer pages where you need to build momentum.

1. **Attention** — Bold claim or surprising stat. Stop the scroll.
2. **Interest** — Details that make them lean in. How does it work?
3. **Desire** — Social proof, results, transformation. Make them want it.
4. **Action** — Clear, specific CTA. Tell them exactly what to do next.

### StoryBrand (Donald Miller)

Best for positioning the customer as the hero.

1. **Hero** — The customer (NOT you). They have a problem.
2. **Problem** — External (practical), internal (emotional), philosophical (moral).
3. **Guide** — You. Show empathy + authority.
4. **Plan** — 3 simple steps to success.
5. **Call to Action** — Direct ("Start free trial") or transitional ("Download guide").
6. **Success** — Paint the transformation.
7. **Failure** — What happens if they don't act.

## Section Templates

### Hero Section

```
Headline:     [Value proposition in 10 words or less]
Subheadline:  [Expand on HOW you deliver that value, 20-30 words]
CTA:          [Action verb + outcome] — "Start building" not "Learn more"
Trust bar:    [Social proof: logos, numbers, or micro-testimonial]
```

**Headline formulas:**
- `[Verb] + [outcome] + [without/in/for] + [objection handler]`
  "Ship features daily without touching your CI pipeline"
- `The [noun] that [surprising benefit]`
  "The deploy tool that gives your team 2 days back every week"
- `[Audience]: [bold promise]`
  "Engineering teams: Deploy in seconds, not hours"

**Headline tests:**
- Does it pass the "so what?" test?
- Would you click on it?
- Can you understand the value in 3 seconds?
- Is it specific enough to differentiate?

### Problem Section

```
Section headline:  [Name the pain directly]
Body:              2-3 specific scenarios your audience recognizes
Emotional hook:    How does this FEEL? (frustrated, behind, overwhelmed)
Transition:        "There's a better way" or "It doesn't have to be this way"
```

### Solution/Features Section

```
Section headline:  [How you solve it — benefit-first]
Feature cards:     3-5 max (more = decision fatigue)
Each card:         Icon + benefit headline + 1-2 sentence explanation
Pattern:           [What it does] → [Why you care]
```

**Feature copy formula:**
- ❌ "AI-powered analytics engine with real-time data processing"
- ✅ "See what's working in real-time. Our analytics surface the insights that move the needle."

### Social Proof Section

```
Testimonials:    Quote + Name + Title + Company + (headshot if possible)
Metrics:         3 big numbers (customers, uptime, revenue generated)
Logos:           6-12 recognizable brands in a trust bar
Case studies:    Result headline + 2-sentence story + "Read more"
```

**Testimonial formula:**
"Before [product], we [old painful way]. Now we [new better way]. [Specific result]."

### How It Works

```
3 steps maximum. If you need more, simplify your product.
Step 1: [Simple action verb] — "Connect your repo"
Step 2: [Simple action verb] — "Configure your pipeline"
Step 3: [Simple action verb] — "Ship with confidence"
```

### CTA Section (Final)

```
Headline:     Restate the transformation (not the product)
Subheadline:  Handle the #1 objection (free trial, no credit card, cancel anytime)
Primary CTA:  High-contrast button with action text
Secondary:    Lower-commitment alternative ("Talk to us" / "See a demo")
```

### FAQ Section

Answer the top 5-7 objections disguised as questions:
- Pricing/cost concerns
- Implementation time
- Security/compliance
- Integration with existing tools
- Support/reliability
- Comparison to alternatives

**FAQ copy rule:** Answer confidently. No hedging. "Yes, we integrate with X" not "We're working on X integration."

## Voice Principles

### Clarity Over Cleverness
- ❌ "Synergize your operational workflows with AI-driven paradigms"
- ✅ "Automate the boring parts so your team can build"

### Specific Over Generic
- ❌ "Save time and money"
- ✅ "Cut deploy time from 45 minutes to 12 seconds"

### Active Over Passive
- ❌ "Results are delivered by our platform"
- ✅ "Our platform delivers results"

### Confident Over Hedging
- ❌ "We can help you potentially improve..."
- ✅ "We improve your deploy speed by 10x"

### Conversational Over Corporate
- ❌ "Our enterprise-grade solution leverages..."
- ✅ "We built this because deploys shouldn't take an hour"

## CTA Best Practices

**Primary CTA (high commitment):**
- "Start building" / "Get started free" / "Deploy now"
- High contrast, large button, above the fold AND at the bottom

**Secondary CTA (low commitment):**
- "See how it works" / "Watch demo" / "Talk to us"
- Outline button or text link, paired with primary

**CTA tests:**
- Does it tell them what happens next?
- Is the verb active? (no "Submit" or "Click here")
- Is the objection handled nearby? ("No credit card required")

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ Multiple competing CTAs on the same section
- ❌ "We" focused copy — flip to "you" focused
- ❌ Feature-first instead of benefit-first
- ❌ Walls of text without visual breaks
- ❌ Vague headlines ("Welcome to our platform")
- ❌ "Learn more" as a CTA (learn more about WHAT?)
- ❌ Fake urgency ("Only 3 spots left!" when there aren't)
- ❌ Jargon your audience doesn't use
- ❌ More than 3 steps in "How it works"
- ❌ Testimonials without names/titles (anonymous = not credible)

## Gotchas

1. **Write the headline last.** Write the body first, then distill the headline from what you wrote. The best headlines come from understanding the full argument.
2. **One idea per section.** If a section is trying to do two things, split it.
3. **Read it out loud.** If you stumble, rewrite.
4. **The fold matters less than momentum.** The real goal isn't cramming everything above the fold, it's making people want to keep scrolling.
5. **Social proof placement matters.** Put it right after the claim it validates. "10x faster deploys" → immediately followed by a testimonial about speed.
6. **Mobile copy is shorter.** What works on desktop can overwhelm on a phone. Test at 375px width.
